**The gap:** every piece exists in isolation but nobody combines them. Promptfoo and skill-creator do pass-rate evals *for* skills but don't publish/distribute or version-pin against specific model releases as a first-class registry concept. GitHub's `gh skill` and Portkey's Skills Registry do publish/distribute/version SKILL.md but have **zero eval or pass-rate measurement** — publishing is gated only by spec-validation and security scanning, not by "does this skill actually work." Braintrust/Langfuse/PromptLayer have mature eval+regression infrastructure but are prompt-object-centric, with no SKILL.md awareness at all. **No product found does version-pinned pass-rate measurement gating a SKILL.md publish step, with automatic re-validation triggered specifically by underlying model changes** — that combination (guarded publish + model-change-triggered regression, specifically for agent skills as a package format) appears to be open territory. No YC-funded "agent skill management" startup surfaced in search; closest signals are official-vendor features (GitHub, Anthropic, Portkey) rather than independent startups, and one thin OSS ecosystem (agentskills.io tooling) with no visible traction metrics.

All findings above are from search snippets + direct WebFetch of primary pages, except skills.re (blocked, unverified) and the darkrishabh eval-runner (traction unconfirmed).
