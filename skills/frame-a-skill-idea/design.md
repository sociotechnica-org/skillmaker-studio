# Design — Frame A Skill Idea

## Intent
Lays the foundation for a skill: turns a director's raw idea into a
**researchable statement of intent** — one clear job worth building. It serves
a director whose idea is too big, too thin, or too fuzzy to research — or
already solid. It is *not* for settling how the skill works (research and
drafting own that), and *not* for doing thinking the idea does not need. The
skill is the judgment of *when* to harden an idea (Scope), grow it
(Brainstorm), or stand back.

## When to use / triggers
Reach for it when a new idea can't yet hold the weight of research — the job is
too vague to research, or reads sharp but hides a soft core. The bar it hits: a
researcher can act on the idea without asking "what does this even do?", and a
stranger could tell when to reach for the finished skill.
